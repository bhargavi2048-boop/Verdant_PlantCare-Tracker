Plant Care Tips

Learn how to keep your plants thriving all year long

💧

The Finger Test

Before watering, stick your finger 2–3 cm into the soil. If it feels dry, water. If moist, wait. This simple test beats any schedule for most plants.

Watering
🚿

Water Deeply, Not Often

Water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom. This encourages roots to grow deep. Shallow, frequent watering keeps roots near the surface and weakens the plant.

Watering
☀️

Rotate Your Plants

Give your pot a quarter turn every week so all sides receive equal sunlight. This prevents lopsided growth and keeps your plant looking symmetrical and healthy.

Light
❄️

Room Temperature Water

Always use room-temperature water. Cold water shocks tropical plant roots and can cause leaf drop or stunted growth. Leave tap water overnight — it also lets chlorine evaporate.

Watering
🪟

Understanding Windows

South-facing windows give the most light. East gets gentle morning sun. West gets strong afternoon sun. North is lowest light — perfect for shade-lovers like ferns.

Light
🪨

Drainage is Everything

Always use pots with drainage holes. Sitting in water causes root rot — the #1 killer of houseplants. Add a layer of pebbles or use well-draining potting mix.

Soil
🐛

Spot Pests Early

Check leaves weekly — top and underside. Common pests: spider mites (tiny dots), mealybugs (white fluff), fungus gnats (tiny flies). Catching them early makes treatment simple.

Pests
🧴

Neem Oil Solution

Mix 2 tsp neem oil + 1 tsp dish soap + 1L water. Spray on leaves weekly as a natural pesticide and fungicide. Safe for most plants and highly effective.

Pests
🍂

Winter Adjustments

Most plants need less water in winter as growth slows. Reduce watering frequency by 30–50%. Keep plants away from cold drafts and heating vents which dry the air.

Seasonal
🌸

Spring Repotting

Spring is the best time to repot. Choose a pot only 2–5 cm larger than the current one. Too large and the soil stays wet too long, risking rot.

Seasonal
🌱

Feed in Growing Season

Fertilise every 2–4 weeks in spring and summer. Use a balanced liquid fertiliser diluted to half strength. Never fertilise in winter — plants are resting.

Soil
🌡️

Watch the Temperature

Most houseplants thrive at 15–25°C. Sudden temperature changes stress plants. Keep them away from AC vents, open windows in winter, and cold glass panes.
